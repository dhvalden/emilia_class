Extracting Structured Data from Text

A study into the evolution of gangs in London

  • Source material: unstructured text descriptions
  • Goal: extract meaningful, structured data
  • Domain: gang activity and development across London

The Challenge: Contextual Awareness

Gangs may be referenced under different names across sources — yet refer to the same entity.

  • 🔗 Rule-based matching — aliases and keyword lists; brittle and hard to maintain
  • 📊 Entity resolution algorithms — graph-based or clustering approaches; limited by structure
  • 🧠 Knowledge bases — manual curation; slow and expensive to scale
  • ✅ Large Language Models (LLMs) — understand context, nuance, and ambiguity naturally; the most powerful approach for this problem

The Challenge: Information Preservation

Free text is rich with detail — capturing it all requires a sophisticated data model.

  • 📅 Timelines — tracking how gangs evolve over time
  • 🤝 Mergers & splits — relationships between gang factions
  • 🔀 Cross-references — links between entities across sources
  • ⚡ Incidents — events, conflicts, and activities
  • 📍 Geolocation — territorial presence and movement

➡️ This demands a complex, relational database system capable of capturing multi-dimensional relationships.

The Solution: Relational Database

A structured, relational database designed to preserve the full richness of the source material.

  • 🗄️ Captures complexity — timelines, incidents, relationships, and geography all stored relationally
  • 🔍 Queryable structure — enables precise, repeatable analysis across the dataset
  • 📈 Longitudinal analysis — track how gangs and their networks evolve over time
  • 🕸️ Network mapping — uncover connections, hierarchies, and shifting alliances

➡️ A foundation for evidence-based insight into gang evolution across London.

Recap: LLMs Doing What They Do Best

Using AI for information compression — transforming unstructured text into structured, queryable data.

  • 🤖 LLMs as extractors — converting free text into structured tables at scale
  • 📦 Information compression — distilling rich narratives into meaningful, relational data
  • ⚡ Increased volume — AI enables processing far more source material than manual methods
  • ✅ Human verification is essential — results must always be checked, validated, and reviewed

➡️ AI accelerates the work — human judgement ensures its quality.
